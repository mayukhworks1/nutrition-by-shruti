03 — Reference Sites — Real Research

What we studied.
What we're taking.

Every insight here comes from actually visiting these sites and reading what they do — their exact words, their specific design decisions, and how we translate each one for Shruti's brand.

Function Health
functionhealth.com ↗
"It's time you own your health. 160+ lab tests and personalized protocols for instant action."
Why it works
Their headline is a provocation: "100 Healthy Years" — not "better health" but a specific, bold number. Forces the visitor to reconsider their ambitions.
Medical Board listed with full credentials — Harvard, Stanford, Cleveland Clinic, Columbia. Each name links. It's a roster, not a logo wall.
Case studies are person-first: "Function like Aimee — The cancer signals doctors missed." The person is the hero. The technology is the tool.
Comparison table: "Not your average checkup" side-by-side vs standard GP visit. 14 specific features. Makes the gap viscerally clear.
What we take for Shruti
Replace "Book Now" with story-first CTAs: "See How Shilpa Shah's Family of 7 Changed" — person-first case studies on the website.
Add a comparison table: OligoScan vs standard blood test. 21 minerals vs 0. 15 heavy metals vs 0. Non-invasive vs needle. Makes it inarguable.
Feature Shruti's credentials like Function features their board — Stanford listed with exact programme name, not just the word "Stanford".
Organimo
organimo.com ↗
"From body to mind. Limitless begins here. The only natural multivitamin you will ever need."
Why it works
The loading screen is part of the brand: "atlas.png" loads, then "complete" — they make you wait and it builds anticipation. Premium products make you feel the weight of entering.
"The real limitless pill" — they own a single bold claim and say it without apology. They don't list 20 benefits first. They make the promise, then prove it.
Scroll narrative: "Journey into the wonderful world of Organimo" — they call the homepage a journey. Each benefit is a destination, not a bullet point.
Tag strip: "vegan · harvested in canada · non gmo · gluten free · no preservatives · made in usa" — marquee of trust signals that scrolls continuously.
What we take for Shruti
Shruti needs ONE big bold claim on the homepage — not 6 services. "The only OligoScan practitioner in India." Say it big, say it first.
Scrolling trust-signal bar: "Stanford School of Medicine · UC Berkeley · CE-Certified OligoScan · Mumbai · Delhi · 500+ Clients" — always visible.
Treat the homepage as a journey, not a menu. Each section is a step in understanding what Shruti does — Problem → Technology → Process → Results → Book.
WHOOP
whoop.com ↗
"Backed by PHDs, worn by MVPs. Daily WHOOP wear is linked to 91 more minutes of weekly activity, 2.3 more hours of sleep per week."
Why it works
Every claim has a number: NOT "improves sleep" but "2.3 more hours of sleep per week". NOT "reduces sick days" but specific member outcome data. Specificity = credibility.
Celebrity + regular person alternated: Cristiano Ronaldo → "Ashlynn P., WHOOP member". World-class social proof next to everyday proof. Both are real.
Three tier membership (One/Peak/Life at $199/$239/$359) with clear feature differentiation — each tier has a personality, not just a price. "Peak" implies aspiration.
Dark UI for the data sections — when showing heart rate, recovery scores, strain data, the background goes to pure black. Data is serious. Design reflects that.
What we take for Shruti
Every metric on the homepage must have a SPECIFIC NUMBER: not "helps with diabetes" but "1.7 Pt average HbA1c drop in clients". Not "better sleep" but "92% report deeper sleep".
Two-tier social proof: Shruti's credentials (Stanford, Berkeley) beside a named client: "Sidhant Mehta → 8 parameters brought to normal range." Both levels of proof together.
Dark section for the OligoScan UI — black background, amber/green/red status indicators, monospace type for the data readout. Signal: this is clinical, not casual.
Dr Mark Hyman
drhyman.com ↗
"The most trusted name in functional medicine. 15x NYTimes Best Seller. Personalized, 360 functional medicine for your well-being."
Why it works
Content categories are health goals, not service names: "Longevity / Brain Health / Gut Health / Metabolic Health / Hormone Health / Detoxification" — the visitor finds themselves in the category, not in the service list.
Blog titles are specific and actionable: "Why Your Cholesterol Is High—and How to Start Fixing It in 10 Days" — not "Nutrition Tips". The specificity makes you feel Hyman understands your problem personally.
Content IS the authority — he doesn't just list credentials, he publishes 1150+ podcast episodes. The volume of expertise is itself the social proof.
"Ask AI Mark" — an AI trained on his books and clinical experience. He has turned his knowledge into a product. It's always available, always on-brand.
What we take for Shruti
Rename nav categories from service names to health goals: Instead of "15 Day Gut Reset", use "I have gut & digestion problems" — visitor-first navigation.
Rename blog article titles to be specific and outcome-oriented. "Why I Wouldn't Recommend Ensure" is already perfect. The Ensure article title should appear on the homepage as a preview — it signals expert confidence.
Build a content hub around 5 health goals: Gut Health · Hormones · Diabetes/Metabolic · Detox · Longevity. Each with 3–4 free articles and one paid programme.
ZOE
zoe.com ↗
"Feel healthier. In weeks. Science is our superpower. Now it's yours. The world's largest nutrition study."
Why it works
"Feel healthier. In weeks." — a specific TIME promise. Not "feel better eventually". In. Weeks. This is what converts a hesitant visitor into a buyer.
Member stories use REAL first names with outcome headlines: "Becky — I finally broke free from years of yo-yo dieting." "Travis — I empirically have data that shows I'm improving." Name + specific outcome = believable.
"Science is our superpower. Now it's yours." — the copy democratises the science. They're not gatekeeping expertise, they're handing it to the customer.
"Life on ZOE" — they gave the membership experience a brand name. You don't just "use ZOE", you live on ZOE. Community identity is built into the product copy.
What we take for Shruti
Add a time promise: "Most clients notice a change within 2 weeks." This directly mirrors Shruti's own data — "80% feel better digestion in 2 weeks" is already on the site.
Named client outcome cards: "Sidhant Mehta — Brought 8 parameters to normal range." "Shilpa Shah — Entire family of 7 now her clients." Short, specific, first name visible. No long paragraph walls.
Give the OligoScan experience a brand name: "The Shruti Protocol" — a 4-step experience (Scan → Results → Consult → Track). Make it feel like a product, not a service.
Ritual
ritual.com ↗
"We believe you deserve to know what you're putting in your body. And why." Radical transparency as brand identity.
Why it works
Every ingredient has a visible source, country, and reason: "Magnesium — from a farm in Utah — because most people are deficient." Transparency is their entire marketing strategy.
The packaging IS the product: yellow capsules with visible "beadlets" inside — you can see the ingredients. The design says "nothing to hide" without saying it.
Their tone rejects wellness cliché: "We don't believe in magic. We believe in science." They explicitly distance themselves from the woo-woo supplement space.
What we take for Shruti
OligoScan transparency section: show the ACTUAL scan output. "Your scan shows 21 numbers, in real time, on a tablet. Here's what one looks like." Demystify the process = reduce booking hesitation.
No wellness clichés in the copy. Replace "wellness journey" with "diagnostic protocol". Replace "holistic" with "root cause". Replace "nourish" with "correct". Shruti's tone = clinical + warm.
04 — Design System

Colours · Type · Tone

Every colour, font, and word choice is chosen for a reason. Here's the system — and why each decision was made for Shruti specifically.

Colour Palette — with rationale
Warm Cream
#F5EFE0
Page canvas. Warmer than white — medical without sterile. Aged paper = knowledge + trust.
Deep Espresso
#2C1F14
Headlines, dark sections. Warm black — premium, authoritative. Not corporate black.
Terracotta
#C4622D
Every CTA, accent line, stat numeral. Warm urgency. Earthy — connects nutrition with soil, food, nature.
Near-Black
#0D0D0D
OligoScan data UI background only. Black = clinical instrument. Signals: this is real technology.
Amber Gold
#C9A84C
Data labels inside dark sections only. Like a readout display. Warm precision.
Warm Sage
#6B7C5C
Secondary CTAs, "Optimal" status badges. Connects health with nature without screaming "wellness".
