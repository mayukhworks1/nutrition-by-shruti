01 — Current State

What Is Broken
and Why It Matters

The current site at nutritionbyshruti.com was audited across every section, every interaction point, and every conversion moment. Here is what we found.

"We help you fix what your doctor couldn't using Functional Medicine" — this line lives on the current site. It is buried under a floating heading with no support, no design, and no CTA. It is the strongest line on the page, and it is invisible.

02 — Strategic Positioning

Disease-Centric vs.
Data-Centric

Traditional medical websites list conditions and tell you to call a doctor. The platforms billion-dollar wellness brands are building — Levels, Function Health, Forward — are data-centric: they show you what your body is actually doing. Shruti has the OligoScan. That changes everything.

Today — Disease-Centric

"Nutrition Consultation Services"

  • Lists health conditions (Diabetes, Gut Issues)
  • Asks you to book a call with a nutritionist
  • Explains services as features, not outcomes
  • Authority comes from credentials only
  • OligoScan buried in announcement bar
  • Multiple pages, fragmented journey
→
Tomorrow — Data-Centric

"Precision Functional Health Platform"

  • Shows you what your body's data reveals
  • Invites you into a diagnostic journey
  • Services lead with outcomes and transformation
  • Authority comes from results + technology
  • OligoScan is the hero of the experience
  • Single-scroll narrative, zero friction
"Shruti uses technology that reads 20+ mineral and heavy metal markers from a palm scan in 2 minutes. The same data-driven philosophy that built WHOOP and Levels into category leaders. This is not a wellness blog. This is a diagnostic practice."
03 — Reference Sites

Why We Chose These.
What We Take From Each.

Every reference was chosen for a specific design decision — not general mood board inspiration. Each one maps to something we will do on Shruti's site.

Reference 01 · Premium Visual Entry
Organimo
Sea Moss & Bladderwrack — "You Are Limitless" · Wellness Supplement Brand
↗ organimo.com
"FROM BODY  ·  TO MIND  ·  Limitless begins here."
Why It Works

Opens with a cinematic dark preloader — a geometric gold triangle on near-black — before any content loads. The site communicates premium before a single claim is made. Scroll-driven narrative then unfolds the product story across one long page: the problem, the solution, the science, the invitation.

The headline "The real limitless pill" is provocative and memorable. It positions the product before the rational brain catches up.

What We Take for Shruti
Entry Point
The first impression is a design decision. A confident visual entry — a full Shruti portrait, or a dramatic opening line — sets the frame. The current greyed-out stock photo sets the wrong frame.
Brand Line
Shruti needs one line that owns her positioning. Like "Limitless begins here," we propose: "We find what your doctor missed." One line. No hedging.
Palette
Near-black + gold accent = two-tone discipline. Every element serves the system. Shruti's palette should be equally controlled — Warm Cream + Espresso + Terracotta. No accidental green buttons.
Reference 02 · Clinical Authority
Function Health
100 Lab Tests. Real-Time Health Data. "100 Healthy Years." · Diagnostic Health Platform
↗ functionhealth.com
Dark · Full-bleed data UI · Large sans-serif type · Lab result cards floating on black
Why It Works

Function Health doesn't talk about health — it shows you health data. The homepage features clean modular cards of actual lab results — glucose, cortisol, testosterone — displayed against a dark, clinical interface. The copy replaces jargon with clear outcome statements: "Know your numbers. Own your future."

Every design decision signals authority through data, not through a doctor's headshot. Whitespace is extreme and intentional — it signals that what they do is precise.

What We Take for Shruti
Card System
Replace every carousel and slider with a static three-pillar card grid: Diagnostics · Gut Reset · Longevity. Each card: one outcome statement, one CTA. Nothing more.
Copy Shift
"Fix your gut" → "Unlock your metabolic baseline." Language that speaks to an educated, executive client who wants precision — not soft wellness reassurance.
Whitespace
Sparse, perfectly aligned layouts feel expensive. Cluttered sites feel cheap. Every section in the new Shruti site should breathe — padding is not wasted space, it is a trust signal.
Reference 03 · Data as Visual Language
Levels Health
"See How Food Affects Your Health." · CGM + Metabolic Data Platform
↗ levels.com
Dark mode · Glucose curve charts · Green metabolic data · High-contrast UI · No stock photos
Why It Works

Levels uses the interface itself as the product hero. Instead of photos of people eating salads, the hero is a glucose response chart — a real data readout showing how a meal affects your blood sugar in real time. The dark-mode UI with green data accents mimics scientific instrumentation.

This is the principle: if you make data beautiful, you make science accessible. Visitors don't need to understand biochemistry — they just need to feel that this brand does.

What We Take for Shruti
OligoScan UI
The OligoScan section on the new site should display a high-fidelity mockup of an actual scan report — minerals, heavy metals, oxidative stress — rendered on a near-black background. The data is the hero image.
Dark Mode Section
One full dark section in an otherwise warm-toned site creates contrast and signals "this is where the science lives." Dark mode ≠ the whole site. It signals the diagnostic layer.
Perception Shift
"I am speaking to a nutritionist" → "I am accessing a cutting-edge health lab." That shift happens through how the data is presented — not through what credentials are listed.
Reference 04 · Health Optimisation Brand
WHOOP
"Unlock You." · Wearable Performance & Recovery Platform
↗ whoop.com
All-black · Recovery score readouts · Bold white type · Athletic editorial photography · "Know Yourself Better"
Why It Works

WHOOP sells a $30/month subscription wristband to athletes, executives, and health obsessives. Their site succeeds by making health data feel like a competitive advantage, not a medical concern. The tone is aspirational and performance-driven: "Unlock your potential. Recover faster. Perform at your peak."

The photography is editorial — athletes mid-motion, data overlaid. Nothing soft. Nothing generic. It signals that health is something you actively pursue, not passively receive from a doctor.

What We Take for Shruti
Tone
Shift from "care for yourself" to "optimise yourself." Shruti's client — an urban professional managing diabetes or PCOS — wants to feel in control, not managed. Language should reflect that.
Photography
No stock. No yoga poses. Real photography of real people in real life — cooking, working, running — with subtle data overlays or OligoScan visuals in the frame.
Positioning
"Health as performance" attracts high-value clients who invest in results. Frame Shruti's consultation not as a medical appointment but as accessing a personal health system.
Reference 05 · Concierge Medicine UX
Forward Health
"The Future of Healthcare." · AI-Powered Primary Care Clinic
↗ goforward.com
All-white · Extreme whitespace · Sans-serif clinical type · Modular layout · "Apply" not "Sign Up"
Why It Works

Forward positions its healthcare as so premium and selective that you don't "sign up" — you apply. The intake form is framed as an application process. This single UX decision creates the perception of exclusivity, pre-qualifies leads, and eliminates time-wasters before the first call.

The site itself is almost brutally minimal — white background, clean type, one thing per screen. It signals: we are so confident in what we do that we don't need to persuade you.

What We Take for Shruti
Application Flow
Replace "Book a free call" with a short intake questionnaire framed as "Tell us about your health." 5 questions → personalised response → booking. Pre-qualifies, establishes value, reduces time-wasters.
Exclusivity
Add a signal: "Shruti accepts [X] new clients per month. [Y] spots available for July." If capacity is limited, say so. Scarcity is honest and it converts.
Minimalism
Clinical modernism = fewer elements, better placed. The goal is not to fill the screen but to own it. Every element on the page should earn its position.
Reference 06 · Scrollytelling Architecture
Apple
apple.com/iphone · Product Presentation & User Experience Design
↗ apple.com/iphone
White · One idea per screen · Scroll-triggered reveal animations · Giant type · No navigation away from the page
Why It Works

Apple doesn't force you through 10 product pages. One long homepage, one scroll. As you move down, the story of the product unfolds through large typography, cinematic imagery, and scroll-triggered animations. By the time you reach the bottom, you understand the product completely — rationally and emotionally — without having clicked a single link.

This reduces cognitive load to near-zero. The visitor is guided. No decisions. No "where do I go next?" The funnel is the page.

What We Take for Shruti
Structure
Consolidate Shruti's multi-page site into one long scroll narrative: The Problem → The Cause → The Diagnosis (OligoScan) → The Protocol → The Transformation → The Invitation.
Animations
Scroll-triggered reveals — elements fading in as you scroll — create the "dopamine of discovery." Visitors stay engaged because something is always happening as they move down the page.
Typography
Large type at key moments. One idea per viewport. The hero headline should be 80–100px minimum — big enough to land like a statement, not a suggestion.
Reference 07 · Expert Personal Brand
Dr. Mark Hyman
"Young Forever. Feel better than ever." · Functional Medicine Thought Leader
↗ drhyman.com
Dark green · Large founder portrait · Bold serif headline · Podcast + Books + Programs navigation · Authority ecosystem
Why It Works

Hyman's site hero is a large, confident portrait of him — not a concept, not a stock image, just the expert looking at you. His entire site is an authority ecosystem: podcast, books, community, programs — all positioned as legitimate entry points. Content and services are woven together seamlessly.

What We Take for Shruti
Founder Portrait
Shruti's face is the brand. Commission a professional shoot. Use a full-viewport portrait in the hero. Make it the first thing visitors see.
Content Funnel
The Journal (blog) must feed into services. Every article should link naturally to the relevant program — gut health article → Gut Reset program. Content is the top of the sales funnel.
Reference 08 · Conversion Optimisation
Noom
"Health for real humans." · Psychology-Based Weight Management App
↗ noom.com
Warm white · Bold serif · Problem-first copy · Quiz as primary CTA · High conversion rate homepage
Why It Works

Noom's homepage primary CTA is not "Book Now" — it is a quiz. "Take our 5-minute quiz to find your personalised plan." The quiz creates active participation (sunk-cost psychology: once you've answered 5 questions, you want to see the results), collects data, and routes visitors to the right product automatically.

What We Take for Shruti
Quiz CTA
Replace "Book Free Call" (high friction cold ask) with "Find out what's keeping you unwell" — a 5-question symptom audit that ends with a personalised recommendation and a booking prompt.
Problem-First
Lead with why diets, supplements, and conventional medicine haven't worked — before presenting Shruti as the answer. Position her as the solution to a known, named frustration.
Reference 09 · Science Meets Warmth
ZOE
"The world's largest nutrition science study." · Personalised Nutrition Platform · Tim Spector
↗ joinzoe.com
Deep blue · Clean sans-serif · Video testimonials · Scientific credibility in approachable language
Why It Works

ZOE balances rigorous science with human warmth. Their testimonial section uses real names, professions, ages, and specific health outcomes — not vague quotes. Video testimonials are embedded and auto-play muted. The science is cited clearly but explained simply.

What We Take for Shruti
Testimonials
Structure as case studies: Name · Age · Condition → Approach → Outcome. Anil Agarwal's HbA1c reversal story should be a headline, not a quote card.
Video
Even one muted auto-play client video is worth ten written quotes. Commission 1–2 short video testimonials (30–60 seconds) for the redesigned site.
Reference 10 · Editorial Wellness Brand
Ritual
"You deserve to know what you're putting in your body." · Science-Backed Supplements
↗ ritual.com
Off-white · Yellow accent · Ingredient transparency · Editorial product photography · "Made Traceable" copy
Why It Works

Ritual's entire brand is built on one UX principle: radical transparency. They show you exactly what is in every capsule, where each ingredient comes from, and why it was chosen. This transparency becomes the brand differentiator. In a market full of vague wellness claims, "we'll show you everything" is the most powerful positioning available.

What We Take for Shruti
Transparency
Show the OligoScan process in detail — what it measures, what the report looks like, what Shruti does with the data. The more specific the process, the more trust it generates.
Ingredients = Methodology
Ritual shows ingredient sources. Shruti should show her methodology — "here is exactly how we design your protocol" — as a step-by-step visual breakdown.

06B — Removals

What We Are
Cutting and Why

Subtraction is a design decision. Every item below is either harming conversions, undermining brand perception, or creating visual noise.

✕
Email popup on page load Fires before a single line is read. Causes 15–25% immediate bounce on mobile. Move to exit-intent.
✕
Green "BOOK NOW" button Visually incoherent with the warm beige/rose palette. Screams template. Replaced with terracotta.
✕
All stock yoga / meditation photography Generic, un-ownable, and interchangeable with thousands of competitor sites. Replaced with original photography.
✕
Every slider and carousel 80%+ of users never interact with carousel controls. Most see only the first slide. Static card grids outperform sliders on every conversion metric.
✕
"Learn More" as a CTA No stakes, no urgency, no direction. Every CTA needs to tell the visitor exactly what happens next when they click.
✕
"Wellness Wisdom" section name Generic and forgettable. Replaced with "The Journal" — editorial, ownable, and authority-building.
✕
WhatsApp auto-open on load Creates visual noise before the visitor has expressed any intent. Trigger only after 30 seconds of dwell time.
✕
Multi-page fragmented navigation Every time a visitor navigates away from the homepage, they exit the conversion funnel. Single-scroll homepage eliminates this.
✕
"Chief Medical Officer" title Confusing for a solo practitioner. Visitors don't know if this is a clinic or a personal brand. Replace with "Founder & Functional Medicine Practitioner."
✕
Bio written as LinkedIn profile "Extensive experience working with..." is a résumé bullet. Replaced with a 3-sentence narrative that leads with story, conviction, and personal connection.
