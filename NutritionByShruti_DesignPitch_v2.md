# Nutrition by Shruti
## Website Experience & Brand Redesign Proposal

**Agency:** The Works · **Date:** June 2026 · **Version:** 2.0

---

## 01. The Core Opportunity

Shruti has UC Berkeley credentials, a clinic relationship with Poly Medicure, measurable clinical outcomes (HbA1c reversed, medications eliminated), and the OligoScan — a technology that reads 20+ mineral and heavy metal markers from your palm in 2 minutes.

**None of that shows up on the current website.**

The site presents Shruti as a nutritionist. The redesign will present her as a **precision health practitioner** — someone whose work is grounded in data, not guesswork.

> **The shift:** from "nutrition consultation" → "personalised functional health diagnosis + transformation"

---

## 02. What's Broken Right Now

| Problem | Impact |
|---|---|
| Email popup fires the moment you land | Kills trust before a single word is read |
| Hero uses stock yoga photography | Zero brand differentiation |
| "Learn More" as the primary CTA | No urgency, no action |
| Stats (200+ clients, 60% medication reduction) are buried in small type | Strongest proof on the site is invisible |
| Services explained as features, not outcomes | Visitor doesn't know what they're buying |
| OligoScan barely mentioned | The single biggest differentiator is hidden |
| Testimonials have blurred photos, no structure | Weakest possible presentation of the best proof |
| Green CTA button clashes with the beige/rose palette | Signals template, not brand |
| 5 nav links + slider carousels + popup + WhatsApp widget | Cognitive overload |

**The root problem isn't aesthetic — it's strategic.** The site is *disease-centric*: it lists conditions and tells you to call someone. The redesign makes it *data-centric*: it shows you how Shruti identifies the root cause of what you're feeling, using real technology, and produces a result that's measurable.

---

## 03. The Repositioning

| Current Perception | Target Perception |
|---|---|
| "A nutritionist I can consult" | "A precision health practitioner I have access to" |
| Soft wellness brand | Clinical authority with human warmth |
| Advice-based practice | Diagnostics → Protocol → Transformation |
| What your doctor recommends | What your doctor missed |

The OligoScan is the anchor of this repositioning. A palm scan that quantifies your mineral deficiencies, heavy metal load, and oxidative stress in real time is not a wellness gimmick — it's the same principle that $300/month platforms like Function Health and Levels have built entire businesses on. Shruti has it. The site doesn't use it at all.

## 04. Design References — What We're Borrowing and Why

Each reference was chosen for a specific design decision, not general inspiration.

---

### Organimo — *Premium Visual Entry Point*
**URL:** organimo.com

Organimo opens with a cinematic dark-mode preloader, a gold geometric mark, and the single line: *"From Body to Mind. Limitless begins here."* You feel premium before you read a single claim.

**What we take:** The idea that the *first impression* is a design decision. A confident visual entry — whether a full Shruti portrait, a cinematic scroll, or a dramatic opening line — sets the frame for everything that follows. The current site's opening (grey photo, loading slowly) sets the wrong frame entirely.

---

### Apple — *Scrollytelling as the Homepage*
**URL:** apple.com/iphone

Apple doesn't have 15 pages explaining a product. They have one long page where the story unfolds as you scroll. Big type. One idea per screen. Triggered reveals. By the time you reach the bottom, you understand the product completely and emotionally.

**What we take:** Shruti's current 6-page website will be consolidated into a single narrative homepage. As the visitor scrolls, they experience: **The Problem → The Cause → The Diagnosis (OligoScan) → The Protocol → The Transformation → The Invitation.** One funnel. No branching. No cognitive load.

---

### Levels Health & WHOOP — *Data as the Visual Language*
**URL:** levels.com · whoop.com

These platforms don't use stock photos of smiling doctors. They use high-contrast dark UI with charts, biometric readouts, and glowing data points. The interface itself signals that the product is science-backed. When you see a glucose curve or a recovery score, you feel you're accessing a lab — not a lifestyle brand.

**What we take:** A dedicated dark-mode section for the OligoScan. Instead of describing the scan in copy, we show a high-fidelity mockup of an actual OligoScan report — mineral levels, heavy metals, oxidative stress — floating on a dark background. *"This is what we know about your body before your first consultation even begins."*

---

### Function Health & Forward — *Clinical Modernism*
**URL:** functionhealth.com · goforward.com

Both sites use extreme whitespace, modular card grids, and sans-serif type to make medicine feel clean, intelligent, and premium. There are no sliders, no popups, no clutter. The design communicates: *we are serious, and we respect your time.*

**What we take:** Replace every slider and carousel on Shruti's site with a static, well-structured card grid. Three core pillars — **Diagnostics, Reset, Longevity** — each presented cleanly with an outcome statement, not a service description. Whitespace is intentional: a sparse, precisely aligned layout signals exclusivity, not emptiness.

## 05. Design System

### Colour

| Role | Colour | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Warm Cream `#F5EFE0` | Backgrounds, breathable sections |
| Primary Dark | Deep Espresso `#2C1F14` | Headlines, dark sections |
| Accent | Terracotta `#C4622D` | CTAs, highlights, key numbers |
| Science | Near-Black `#0D0D0D` | OligoScan section, data UI |
| Data Accent | Amber Gold `#C9A84C` | Data labels, chart elements in dark sections |

### Typography

| Role | Typeface | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Display | Cormorant Garamond | All hero headlines, pull quotes |
| Body | DM Sans | Body copy, labels, UI |
| Data Labels | DM Mono | OligoScan data readouts |

### Tone of Voice

- **Not:** "We offer personalised nutrition plans"
- **Yes:** "We find what your bloodwork missed"

- **Not:** "Fix your gut health"
- **Yes:** "Unlock your metabolic baseline"

Language should speak to a patient who is educated, frustrated with conventional answers, and willing to invest in real answers. Think: senior executive, urban professional, someone who has already tried "eating healthy" and knows that isn't enough.

## 06. New Homepage Architecture

The homepage is a single scroll narrative. No tabs, no sliders, no separate pages for content that belongs in the funnel.

---

**1 — Hero**

Full-screen. Shruti's portrait (left) with a commanding headline (right):
> *"We find what your doctor missed."*

Sub-headline lists 3 proof points: credentials, OligoScan technology, measurable outcomes.
CTA: **Book a Discovery Call** (terracotta button) + *"See our results →"* (secondary text link)

---

**2 — The Problem (Dark Section)**

Large type on dark background. Speak directly to what the visitor is feeling:
> *"You've been told your tests are normal. But you know something is wrong."*

This is the mirror moment. No imagery needed — just type, space, and truth.

---

**3 — The Stats (Impact Bar)**

Four numbers. Large Cormorant numerals in terracotta. Dark or cream background.

`200+` clients · `60%` eliminated medications · `80%` better digestion in 2 weeks · `1.7pt` average HbA1c drop

Each number has a single qualifying line beneath it. Nothing more.

---

**4 — The Approach (3 Pillars)**

Clean card grid. Three columns.

| Diagnostics | Gut Reset | Longevity |
|---|---|---|
| OligoScan + full intake | Root-cause nutrition protocol | Supplement + lifestyle integration |

Each card: pillar name → one outcome line → *"Learn how →"*

---

**5 — OligoScan Section (Dark Mode)**

The standout section. Near-black background, amber accents.

Headline: *"In 2 minutes, we know more about your body than most blood panels reveal."*

Visual: High-fidelity mockup of an OligoScan report — mineral levels, heavy metals, oxidative stress markers — overlaid on the dark background like a scientific instrument readout.

Sub-copy explains what the scan detects and why it changes the consultation entirely. CTA: **Book a Scan** or **Add OligoScan to your consultation**

---

**6 — The Process**

Horizontal timeline. 4 steps. Client-perspective language.

1. *We listen to everything your doctors skipped*
2. *We map the root cause using real diagnostics*
3. *We build a protocol that fits your actual life*
4. *You measure the difference*

---

**7 — About Shruti**

Large portrait. Short narrative bio (3 sentences maximum). Below it: a trust stack — logos/badges: UC Berkeley · Poly Medicure · Functional Medicine Certified.

One pull quote in large italic Cormorant:
> *"Food is the most powerful medicine we have. I'm here to prove it."*

---

**8 — Transformations (Case Studies)**

Replace the current testimonial cards with structured mini case studies:

**Profile → Challenge → Approach → Outcome**

Anil Agarwal, for example: *"HbA1c from 7 to 5.9 in 3 months. Off statins in 6 weeks."* — that is a case study, not a quote. Present it like one.

---

**9 — The Journal (Blog)**

One featured article (large). Two secondary articles beside it. No "Wellness Wisdom" — the section is called **The Journal**. It links back into services.

---

**10 — Closing CTA (Full-Width Dark)**

> *"Your body knows something is wrong. Let's find out what it is."*

One button: **Book Your Free Discovery Call** — terracotta on dark.

## 07. UX Decisions — Action → Reaction

| UX Decision | Why It Works | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| OligoScan presented as a dark-mode, data-UI section | Dark interfaces with data visuals signal scientific precision. The scan stops feeling like a wellness tool and starts feeling like a diagnostic instrument. | Levels, WHOOP |
| Copy shifts from symptom-based to data-based language | "Unlock your metabolic baseline" attracts a different (and higher-value) client than "fix your gut." Language is positioning. | Function Health |
| Single-page scrollytelling replaces multi-page navigation | Removes all friction. The visitor is guided through one persuasive sequence, not asked to explore an unfamiliar site architecture. | Apple |
| Health quiz / symptom audit as primary lead-gen CTA | Converts passive reading into active participation. Sunk-cost psychology: once someone has answered 5 questions, they want to see the results. Far lower friction than a cold "Book Now." | Noom, Forward |
| "Applications" replace contact forms | High-end practices don't have contact forms. A short intake questionnaire pre-qualifies leads and signals that Shruti's time is valuable and limited. | Forward Health |
| Email popup moves from page-load to exit-intent | Page-load popups spike bounce rates. Exit-intent triggers catch visitors who are leaving — the only moment the interruption is tolerable. | CRO standard |
| Sticky mobile bottom bar for booking CTA | The highest-converting mobile UX pattern for service businesses. The CTA stays visible throughout the scroll without disrupting the content. | UI best practice |
| Stats presented at 80–100px type weight | Numbers this size are not read — they are *felt*. A massive "60%" commands attention; the same number in 24px is ignored. | Editorial design |

## 09. Platform Recommendation

| Option | Verdict |
|---|---|
| **Wix (current)** | Cannot achieve the design quality described here. Template-bound, slow image loading, limited animation capability. |
| **Webflow** ✅ | Full design control, clean code, CMS for The Journal, excellent SEO. Recommended for this scope. |
| **Framer** ✅ | Better for scroll-animation-heavy experiences. If the OligoScan section and scrollytelling are priorities, Framer is ideal. |
